Feeling Stuck

 11.16.2022 

I stepped away from learning korean for the last 6 to 8 weeks. Life got busy, so I stopped doing my daily flashcards and meeting with my online tutor. I know I have to get back to it, but I just don’t want to. I’m sure I’ve gotten a bunch of material and the thought of returning to my Korean text book just feels so boring.

The truth is that I haven’t been having much fun lately. My tutor uses the 서울대 한국어 textbook which is a great way to formally learn korean, but topics covered are rarely useful in my day to day life. That, and the book doesn’t explain concepts in English, making it really hard to figure things out on my own.

What I really want is to be able to use Korean when I:

  • speak to my in-laws on the phone;
  • talk to my family at the dinner table; and
  • go to get a haircut at the local Korean hair salon.

So, even though it might not be the wisest decision, I’m going to ditch the text book and create my own study plan.

For my in-laws, I’ll need some go to small talk phrases. Things like how’s your health, what’s the weather like, how’s your golf game. Ten go to questions seems like a good starting point.

For the dinner table, I think phrases like how was school, do you have any homework, and a few way to describe how food taste should work well.

And for the hair salon, I’ve already started that list. If I combine it with my “in-law small talk list” I should be in a good place.
