하세요 vs. 해 주세요

 12.01.2022 

해 주세요 = Please do this for me.
하세요 = Do this, please.
부탁합니다 = Thank you for agree to do this favor for me.

하세요 is used to order someone to do something. It’s literal translation is “please do” but it’s a very direct way of communicating. In English it would be like saying, “do this…please”. “하세요” is something you might hear when your boss asks you to perform a task. Add a verb at the beginning to give more specific orders.

Examples:

  • Verb + 하세요 = Please do verb
  • 설거지 하세요 = Wash the dishes, please.
  • 전화 하세요 = Call me, please

해 주세요 is a direct way of asking someone to do something for you. The literal translation is also “please do” but it’s a slighly less direct way of communicating. As with 하세요, you can add a verb at the beginning to give more specific instructions.

Examples:

  • Verb + 해 주세요 = Please verb for me.
  • 발리 해주세요 = Please do it quickly.
  • 천천히 해주세요 = Please do it slowly
  • 예약해주세요 = Please make a reservation.

부탁합니다 is used to ask for a favor but is commonly used after it is understood the person is going to do the task and when the asker is leaving. A rough literal translation is “Thank you for doing this for me, I will leave you to do it.”
