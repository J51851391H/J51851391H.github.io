Study Plan

 published on 12.02.2022
Start by building a foundation of: 10 to 20 useful phrases that can be used daily; 400 to 500 most frequent nouns, verbs, and adverbs; question words; and 2 or 3 sentence strucutures. Follow a few simple maxims: Consistency is paramount. Each day: (1) 30 minutes of focused study; and (2) language exposure. Learn only what you can use now. Avoid specialized/sophisticated words. Everyday read, write, and speak. Create simple routines to make studying easy.

하세요 vs. 해 주세요

 published on 12.01.2022
해 주세요 = Please do this for me. 하세요 = Do this, please. 부탁합니다 = Thank you for agree to do this favor for me. 하세요 is used to order someone to do something. It’s literal translation is “please do” but it’s a very direct way of communicating. In English it would be like saying, “do this…please”. “하세요” is something you might hear when your boss asks you to perform a task.

음료 vs. 음료수

 published on 11.19.2022
음료 = drink or beverage 음료수 = tasty beverage Not all Koreans agree that there’s a distinction between 음료 and 음료수. Most that I’ve spoken to claim, that the words are interchangable in most situations. But, all agreed that 음료 is a general term that refers to all kinds drinks and beverages. Things like water, beer, wine, coffee, tea, etc. For some, 음료수 is only used to refer to a drink that quenches thurst.

물고기 vs. 생선

 published on 11.18.2022
물고기 = fish (living fish) 생선 = fish (to eat) 해산물 = seafood Both 물고기 and 생선 have the literally meaning of fish but they’re used in different contexts. 물고기 refers to a living fish and is used to refer to a broad range of swimming animials with spines and gills. Things like sharks, tuna, goldfish, etc. But a jellyfish or a starfish. 생선 refers to fish you eat. More specially, fish that have purposely been cuaght for eating, like the salmon on your dinner plate.
